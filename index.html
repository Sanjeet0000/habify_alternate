Habify in Focus

We live in very peculiar times. Our preceding generations were plagued by countless illnesses that couldn’t be cured due to a lack of scientific advancement. While technology has now made it possible to cure even the deadliest disease, the present generation suffers an even graver danger: Mental Illness. According to the CDC, worldwide, one in eight people suffer from a diagnosed mental illness. In the USA, it’s one in four, and in India, it’s one in fourteen. Our country sees a student committing suicide every hour. In 2020 alone, India saw 1.53 lakh people die due to suicide; that’s equal to the number of victims that succumbed to COVID-19. And this is only the tip of the iceberg. Now, the first question you may ask is: “Why didn’t they get any help?” Well, there are two main reasons for this.

  • People don’t want to seek help.
  • People can’t get help.

According to research, a staggering 47% of Indians believe that mental illnesses didn’t require additional treatment; a further 26% were afraid to be diagnosed with one. Then what about the minority of people that wanted to get treatment, you ask? Simply put, they couldn’t. The number of psychiatrists in India is a miniscule 9000. A population of 141 crore, (of which more than 60% is below the age of 35 and sometimes the sole breadwinner of a family) has only 1 mental health professional for every 11,031 people. To put that into perspective, during the second wave of COVID-19, there was only one doctor available for every 1,500 patients. The medical system in India was in shambles overnight. Imagine one doctor looking after 10 times as many patients, all at once. This is not a hypothetical situation. Experts believe that if the mental health scene in India doesn’t change, more than 80% of the population will suffer from a diagnosable mental illness.

Our approach is different from what is currently out there. If mental health was a horizon between point A and point Z, we need the intervention of a counsellor, psychologist, therapist, or psychiatrist somewhere around point K. Habify tries to address the issue before a person reaches point K. Through the Emotional First Aid Academy, we create a frontline of Emotional First Aid providers called Enablers.

Ask yourself: Have you, or anyone close to you, experienced mental stress, loneliness, or worse? If you have, then you can understand why Habify is here to make a positive impact on the world and the people in it.

— THE URGENCY

At the Forefront of Mental Wellness

Modern problems require modern solutions. Since the traditional systems of communication and community seem to have dissipated, we see a startling rise in the cases of mental health issues.

We are passionate about bringing conversations back into the fold. At the EFA Academy, the idea is to stem the tide of loneliness with a tête-à-tête. These private interactions not only provide a much-needed confidante but also stand to break the taboo of talking about mental health.

— OUR BELIEF

We are Unparalleled

While we believe that technology can allow us to achieve more in every aspect of life, we firmly believe that human connection and compassion remain crucial when it comes to healing the mind. There is no doubt that there is a growing recognition within the international community that mental health is to be treated as a non-communicable condition. At Habify, we keep it simple. We focus on the strengths rather than deal with the weaknesses of the mind.

— OUR QUEST

Connecting People

Not everyone needs therapy, but all of us at some point need someone to talk to. Even though technology is more ingrained in every phase of life, we are not advocating that those innovations and technologies should replace human intervention. On the contrary, we envision the adoption of technology as a supplement—to ensure that anyone can find a trusty person to confide in and share their everyday stories, nuggets, and the burden of being human—at an affordable cost.

— THE HABIFY PEOPLE

Breaking the Taboo

We’re a bunch of people who identify as ‘humans’. Together, we are makers, thinkers, foodies, habit-builders and bathroom singers. We approach work and play with curiosity and experimentation (sometimes with sarcasm and wit), using what we learn to create a meaningful community that connects with people, just like you. We believe that mental health is a birth right. Each day we strive to do our part to make the world a little less lonely. After all, our minds are the only place in which we truly live.
